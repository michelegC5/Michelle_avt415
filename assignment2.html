The Arch

Arch is the largest organization that advocates for individuals with disabilities.

Accessibility Audit

Page Title

The title is good as it describes itself as an arc. The whole purpose of the site is to promote and guarantee rights for people with this disability.

Alt Text for Image

All the images on the site provide “alt text”.

Text

Heading: The text is consistent as all the headlines are H1.

Contrast Ratio ("color contrast"): Checking the contrasts ratio showed that the site failed on their normal text and having the ratio be 3.51:1. Their large text was divided with it being WCAG AA Approved but WCAG AAA being a failure. Graphical objects and user interface passed.

Resize Text: The text works well and when at 200% it’s easy to read. There’s no issue with the resize text.


Interaction

Keyboard access and visual focus: You can navigate using the tab to go forward and tab-shift to go backward. Also, the arrow keys work to move up and down the webpage.

Forms, labels, and errors: There isn’t any visible major issue but there seems to be a missing label.


General

Moving, Flashing, or Blinking: There no movement, blinking, or flashing on the site.

Multimedia (video, audio) alternatives,: The site doesn’t have any videos but it an immersive reading that also provides audio to read the page. Plus they have an option for languages from a total of nine.

Basic Structure Check: The structure looks well and there isn’t an issue that I can see.


Issue Found

  • A lot of missing alternative text
  • Linked image missing alternative text
  • Broken Aria Reference
  • Contrast errors
  • Image without title

Recommendations

I believe changing the color of the font and background to make it WCAG Approved. The webpage's layout/design is simple and not over decorative. Just changing the font and fixing the errors will due.
